# Foundations

Before you touch the keyboard, build the mental model. Three short lessons:

1. [**What is a VPS?**](/foundations/what-is-a-vps) — Strip the marketing away. A VPS is just a Linux computer you can reach over the network.
2. [**Anatomy of a Linux Server**](/foundations/anatomy-of-a-server) — Kernel, init system, services, users, ports. The five things on every server.
3. [**Your First SSH Connection**](/foundations/first-ssh-connection) — Log in from this laptop to your Fedora PC. The moment "VPS" stops being abstract.

> **Why a home PC instead of a cloud VPS?** Same operating system, same commands, same skills — but you can break it, unplug it, reinstall it, and try again without spending a cent. When you graduate, paying $5/month for a real VPS will feel obvious instead of intimidating.
